Apollo.io is a different kind of tool than Cleanmails. It's a prospecting platform first, email sender second. It bundles lead databases, enrichment, and outreach into one product.

That bundling is Apollo's strength — and its weakness. Let's break it down.

## What Apollo Actually Is

Apollo is primarily a **B2B lead database** with built-in email sequences. You search for prospects by title, company size, industry, and tech stack — then email them directly from Apollo.

Cleanmails is a **cold email sending infrastructure**. It assumes you already have your leads (from Apollo, LinkedIn, scrapers, or your own lists) and focuses entirely on sending, rotation, validation, and deliverability.

These are different tools solving different problems. But many agencies use Apollo for sending when they shouldn't be.

## Where Apollo Wins

**All-in-one prospecting.** If you don't have leads yet, Apollo's database is genuinely valuable. 275M+ contacts with filters for title, company, tech stack, and intent signals.

**Enrichment.** Upload a list of companies and Apollo fills in contact details. Useful for account-based outreach.

**CRM functionality.** Basic deal tracking and pipeline management built in. Cleanmails is purely an outreach tool.

**LinkedIn integration.** View LinkedIn profiles, track connections, and add LinkedIn steps to sequences.

## Where Cleanmails Wins

**Sending infrastructure.** Apollo's email sending is a secondary feature — it's not built for high-volume outreach. Cleanmails is purpose-built for sending thousands of emails daily with proper rotation, deliverability monitoring, and SMTP management.

**Unlimited sending.** Apollo limits emails per day based on your plan and connected accounts. Cleanmails has no caps — send as much as your infrastructure can handle.

**Built-in SMTP.** Apollo requires you to connect Gmail or Outlook accounts. Cleanmails has its own SMTP engine — no need to buy mailboxes from Google or Microsoft.

**Email validation (unlimited).** Apollo charges credits for email verification. Cleanmails validates every email via SMTP handshake — unlimited, included in the one-time price.

**Cost for agencies.** If you're managing 5+ clients and already have your lead lists, paying $79/user/month for Apollo's sending features makes no sense when Cleanmails does it better for $497 once.

**No vendor dependency.** Your sending reputation, campaign data, and contact lists live on your own server. Apollo owns all of it.

## The Real Question: Do You Need Apollo's Database?

If yes — use Apollo for prospecting, export your leads, and send with Cleanmails. This is what most sophisticated agencies do: use the best tool for each job rather than one mediocre all-in-one.

If no — if you already get leads from LinkedIn Sales Navigator, scraping tools, referrals, or purchased lists — then Apollo's sending features are overpriced for what they offer.

## The Hybrid Approach (Best of Both)

Many agencies use this stack:

1. **Apollo Free** — for prospecting and enrichment (60 credits/month is enough for targeted outreach)
2. **Cleanmails** — for all sending, rotation, validation, and campaign management
3. **Mailreach** ($19/month) — for warm-up

Total cost: $497 one-time + $19/month = far less than Apollo Professional at $79/user/month, with better sending infrastructure.

## Who Should Use Apollo

- Teams that need a lead database and don't have existing lead sources
- Companies doing account-based marketing with intent data
- Solo SDRs sending fewer than 100 emails/day who want everything in one tool
- Teams that need basic CRM functionality alongside outreach

## Who Should Use Cleanmails

- Agencies with existing lead sources (scrapers, LinkedIn, purchased lists)
- Teams sending 500+ emails/day who need proper infrastructure
- Anyone who wants to own their sending reputation and data
- Teams tired of per-seat pricing that scales with headcount
