Instantly is a good product. But at $97–$358/month, it's also an expensive one — especially for agencies managing multiple clients.

Let's do the math, then talk about what the alternative actually looks like.

## The Real Cost of Instantly

Instantly's pricing is per workspace. If you're an agency with 5 clients, you need 5 workspaces.

| Plan | Monthly | Annual | 5 Clients/Year |
|------|---------|--------|----------------|
| Growth | $97 | $1,164 | $5,820 |
| Hypergrowth | $197 | $2,364 | $11,820 |
| Light Speed | $358 | $4,296 | $21,480 |

That's before you factor in the cost of email accounts, domains, and any add-ons.

For a 10-client agency on the Growth plan, you're paying **$11,640/year** just for the software. Every year. Forever.

## What You're Actually Paying For

When you pay Instantly monthly, you're renting:

- Access to their sending infrastructure
- Their warm-up network
- Their campaign management UI
- Their analytics

You don't own any of it. If they raise prices (they have), you pay more. If they change their terms, you adapt. If they go down, your campaigns stop.

## The Self-Hosted Alternative

Self-hosted cold email means you run the software on your own server. You own the infrastructure, the data, and the sending setup.

## What Self-Hosted Requires

Let's be honest about the tradeoffs.

**You need a VPS.** A basic Linux server costs $5–20/month on DigitalOcean, Hetzner, or Vultr. You need one with Port 25 open if you want to use Cleanmails' inbuilt SMTP.

**Initial setup takes time.** Cleanmails includes free installation assistance — the team sets it up on your server end-to-end. But you still need to configure your domains, DNS records, and mailboxes.

**You're responsible for maintenance.** Updates, backups, monitoring. It's not much work, but it's more than zero.

For most agencies, this tradeoff is obvious: a few hours of setup vs. thousands of dollars per year in recurring fees.

## Who Should Switch

Self-hosted makes the most sense if you:

- Run an agency with multiple clients
- Send more than 5,000 emails/month
- Want to own your infrastructure long-term
- Are comfortable with basic server management (or willing to pay for setup)

It's probably not the right move if you're just starting out and sending fewer than 500 emails/month. At that scale, the setup overhead isn't worth it.
